Campaign · 2021

Acceleration For All.

A partnership campaign rebuilding the on-ramp to entrepreneurship — for everyone the old accelerator playbook left out.

Client
ViewSonic × Hustle Fund
Scope
Campaign · Identity · Microsite · Film · Social
Year
2021
Reach
6 regions, global launch from Silicon Valley
The shift

The future of work showed up early.

Lockdowns rewrote the rules of work overnight. Layoffs collided with the largest wave of new-business formation in a decade — passion projects, side hustles, one-person "tiny businesses" starting from kitchens and spare rooms.

The "future of work" wasn't a deck slide anymore. It was happening. And the accelerator playbook — Sand Hill Road, warm intros, six-figure cheques to people who already had them — hadn't caught up.

The brief

Two partners, one missing on-ramp.

ViewSonic and Hustle Fund — a global hardware brand and an early-stage venture fund — wanted to launch a joint accelerator. Hustle Fund needed reach beyond Silicon Valley. ViewSonic wanted to evolve from business supplier to business partner.

Both believed the existing accelerator model was too narrow: too male, too credentialed, too capital-heavy, too geographically concentrated. Our brief was to build the program, the brand, and the experience — and to get founders who'd never see themselves in an accelerator pitch deck to apply anyway.

The insight
The old accelerator was a velvet rope. The new one had to be a starting line.
The move

Treat it as a brand, not a program.

We named it Acceleration For All and built the whole experience as a single piece of campaign IP — a unifying mark, an unflinchingly inclusive cast, and an end-to-end flow stitched together by one promise: Let's help build the future of work by redefining the future of entrepreneurship together.

Pre-launch teasers. A microsite with a low-friction submission flow. Two online qualifying rounds. A hybrid award ceremony broadcast from Silicon Valley. And a sustain layer of post-event content designed to recruit next year's cohort. Same brand, every step.

The work

One brand, every door.

From the opening hype film to a global social system, every touchpoint was built to enrol new founders into the program — and to flatten the wall between watching and entering.

01 · The manifesto

Permission, not pitch.

An opening film and a tagline that gave permission. The message wasn't enter our accelerator — it was follow your pipe dreams. We're here to help.

02 · The identity

A logo that points forward.

Three chevrons for the three stages of the program — gather, reward, celebrate — set against a cast and palette built to read inclusive at any scale, from a 1080×1080 social tile to a 15-foot stage backdrop.

03 · The microsite

A door wide enough to walk through.

A coming-soon page on day one, then a Typeform-driven submission flow, then a public content hub that doubled as the voting platform for the People's Choice award. Every screen was built to lower the cost of applying.

04 · The finalists

Five hustles, five categories.

The inaugural Top 5 — pulled from a global pool spanning tiny business, B2B, social enterprise, and innovation. Each was given brand-level treatment in the run-up to the live final.

05 · The social system

One look. Six regions.

Localised across AZ, DACH, FR, NL, TR, and UK — a single visual system, regionally voiced. The 30-founder grid showed up at every scale, because the brand promise was inclusion and the casting had to carry it.

06 · The live event

Silicon Valley as host. Livestream as front door.

A made-for-livestream award ceremony — built hybrid from inception so the audience watching from a laptop got the same room as the audience in seats. RSVP collateral, merch, and a 30-second sizzle film carried the brand into the room and into the feed.

What it built

A front door, not a one-off.

Acceleration For All gave a hardware brand and an early-stage fund a single, recognisable pop-cultural front door — and a content system that kept paying out long after the awards stage went dark.

What we learned

If the promise is inclusion, every asset has to carry it.

A tagline alone won't do the work. The 30-founder grid wasn't decoration — it was the strategy made visible at every scale. Casting, palette, and the small choices in every social tile carried more weight than any single hero film. Build the brand promise into the smallest unit of work and the biggest unit takes care of itself.
